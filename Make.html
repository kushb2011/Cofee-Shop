French Press Coffee Maker

For the french press, you pour hot water over coarsely ground coffee, wait a few minutes, and press down. Coffee from a French press is bold and heavy, and you don't need to know too much about brewing or extraction techniques to get it right.

Stovetop Coffee Maker

The stove top coffee maker is made for people that would like drinking espresso style coffee. They are mostly inexpensive and easy to use.

Espresso Machine

An espresso machine uses high pressure to force water through coffee within only a few seconds. The espresso that comes out of these machines are the best tasting but the machines themselves are costly

Keurig Machine

The Keurig is the easiest to use on our list of coffee makers. Instead of buying coffee beans or ground coffee, you buy K-Cups and put them in the machine. These cups are filled with ground coffee and are inexpensive.

Drip Coffee Maker

Making coffee with an automatic drip coffee maker is one of the easiest ways to brew a pot of coffee, you just put you coffee grounds in the machine along with your hot water and the machine will make the coffee for you
